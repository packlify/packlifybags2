Flexible Packaging Case Studies — Real Results for Global Brands

Explore how our custom pouch and bag solutions helped brands across coffee, snacks, pet food, and beverages improve shelf presence, reduce packaging costs, and streamline filling operations.

Case Study 1: Specialty Coffee Roaster — 40% Packaging Cost Reduction

Client Background: A mid-size specialty coffee roaster in the Pacific Northwest selling whole bean and ground coffee in 12 oz and 5 lb formats. Previously sourcing stand up pouches and flat bottom bags from two separate US converters at high per-unit cost.

Challenge: Consolidate their pouch formats under one supplier while maintaining the premium matte finish, degassing valve performance, and 12-month shelf life their retail partners required. Target: reduce per-unit cost by at least 25% without sacrificing quality.

Solution: We recommended a PET/AL/PE laminate with registered matte finish and child-resistant zipper for the 12 oz flat bottom bag, and a Kraft/AL/PE structure with woven valve for the 5 lb side gusset bag. Both formats were prototyped with digital samples in 7 days, followed by rotogravure cylinder samples at 12 days. After side-by-side comparison with their existing packs, the client confirmed the barrier data (OTR < 0.1 cc/m²·day) and seal integrity met their spec.

Results: 40% reduction in per-unit pouch cost by consolidating to one manufacturer. 5 lb bag transitioned from flat pouch to side gusset format, increasing shelf billboard area by 35%. Client expanded to 3 additional SKUs within 6 months.

Products used: Flat Bottom Pouches, Side Gusset Bags · Material: PET/AL/PE, Kraft/AL/PE · Features: Registered matte, degassing valve, child-resistant zipper

Case Study 2: Premium Pet Treat Brand — Quad Seal Relaunch with 60% Sales Uplift

Client Background: A US-based premium pet treat brand launching a new line of freeze-dried dog treats. Competing in the super-premium segment against brands with heavy shelf presence in quad seal pouches.

Challenge: Design a shelf-dominant quad seal pouch that communicated "all-natural" and "premium" while providing puncture resistance for irregularly shaped treat pieces. The pouch also needed to run on the client's existing VFFS equipment with minimal line changeover.

Solution: We developed a custom 4-layer laminate (PET/VMPET/NY/PE) delivering both high barrier and puncture resistance at a competitive thickness (110 μ). The print treatment used soft-touch matte finish with spot gloss on the brand logo and ingredient callouts. A laser-scored easy-open feature was integrated above the zipper. Our technical team provided a detailed filling-line compatibility sheet, and the client validated the pouch on their VFFS line with zero seal failures across 5,000 test units.

Results: The new packaging contributed to a 60% increase in retail velocity within the first 90 days post-launch (client-reported). The quad seal format's five-panel design gave the brand 40% more printable surface area compared to their previous stand up pouch. The client awarded Packlify Bags their full US production pipeline for this product line.

Products used: Quad Seal Pouches · Material: PET/VMPET/NY/PE · Features: Soft-touch matte, spot gloss, laser score, resealable zipper

Case Study 3: Cold-Pressed Juice Brand — Spout Pouch Transition from Bottles

Client Background: A European cold-pressed juice brand seeking to transition their 250 ml and 500 ml lines from PET bottles to flexible spout pouches for sustainability messaging and logistics cost reduction.

Challenge: Achieve equivalent shelf life (9 months ambient for pasteurized juice), maintain leak-proof integrity during transport and consumer use, and deliver a sleek, premium look that matched their bottle-packaged brand equity. The client required FDA and EU 10/2011 migration compliance.

Solution: We engineered a PET/AL/NY/PE laminate with an OTR of < 0.05 cc/m²·day and a corner-mounted PP spout with tamper-evident cap. Our team performed drop testing at 1.5 m, burst testing at 400 kPa, and 30-day accelerated shelf-life testing at 40°C/75% RH—all passed without seal failure or delamination. The digital proof captured the brand's signature gradient and metallic accents. Rotogravure production was completed in 18 days for the initial 200,000 unit order.

Results: 55% reduction in packaging weight compared to PET bottles, translating to ~30% logistics cost savings. The spout pouch format was received positively in consumer testing, with 82% of surveyed customers rating it "easy to use and pour." The client expanded to 3 flavor SKUs and added a 1 L refill format within 9 months.

Products used: Spout Pouches · Material: PET/AL/NY/PE · Features: Corner PP spout, tamper-evident cap, metallic print accents
