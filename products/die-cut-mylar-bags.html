Why Die Cut Bags Work

Die cut mylar bags break the rectangular mold of standard flexible packaging. By contour-cutting the pouch perimeter to a custom shape—whether a brand logo silhouette, a category-specific form, or a unique geometric design—die cut bags create instant visual differentiation on shelf. They are frequently used for seasonal launches, limited editions, and premium gift packaging.

  • Custom contour-cut perimeter for unique shelf silhouettes
  • Full-barrier mylar laminate protects product freshness
  • Ideal for seasonal, limited-edition and premium product launches
  • Customizable shape: logo silhouette, category-specific, or abstract design
  • Metallic mylar finish enhances visual impact and product perception

Printing Options

Rotogravure up to 10 colors with Pantone matching. Digital HP Indigo for short runs. Die-cut contour is produced using a custom cutting die; artwork must include the cutting line. We provide a digital proof and cutting-line review before die fabrication. Lead time includes 3-5 additional days for die preparation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does die preparation take?

Die fabrication adds 3-5 business days to the production timeline. We provide a cutting-line review proof before die fabrication begins to ensure the contour matches your design intent.

What shapes are possible with die cut bags?

Any contour that can be cut by a flatbed die is possible: logo silhouettes, category-specific shapes, geometric designs, and custom outlines. Complex shapes with narrow protrusions may require design adjustments for manufacturability.

Is there a minimum quantity for die cut bags?

Flexible MOQ starts around 5,000 units. Die fabrication cost is amortized over the production run, making larger quantities more cost-effective per unit.
