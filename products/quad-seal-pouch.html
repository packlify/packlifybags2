Why Quad Seal Pouches Work

The quad seal pouch is the premium rigid-format flexible package. With four sealed corners and a flat rectangular base, it delivers the shelf presence of a box with the material efficiency of a pouch. Premium snack brands, granola companies, and pet food brands increasingly choose quad seal over traditional bag-in-box formats.

  • Four-corner seal with flat bottom creates a rigid, self-standing box profile
  • Maximum front-panel billboard space with printable side panels
  • Excellent stacking and shelf merchandising in any orientation
  • Laser-scored easy-open with resealable zipper for consumer convenience
  • Runs on automated filling lines with quad-seal pouch-making attachments

Frequently Asked Questions

Are quad seal pouches compatible with my existing filling line?

Quad seal pouches require a filling machine with quad-seal pouch-making or pouch-feeding capability. Many modern VFFS machines support this format. Our technical team provides a compatibility assessment as part of the project review.

What is the difference between quad seal and flat bottom pouches?

Both have flat bottoms, but quad seal pouches have four corner seals creating sharper edges and a more rigid box-like structure. Flat bottom pouches have side gussets for a softer profile. Quad seal is preferred for maximum front-panel impact.

Can I add a resealable zipper to quad seal pouches?

Yes. We integrate press-to-close zippers above a laser-scored easy-open line, allowing consumers to tear open cleanly and reseal for freshness between uses.
