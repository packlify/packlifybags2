Flexible Packaging FAQ — Answers to Common Custom Pouch Questions

MOQ, sampling, lead times, shipping, payment, materials, and quality control—answers to the questions we hear most often from brands exploring custom flexible packaging.

Q: What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for custom pouches?

Our flexible MOQ starts around 5,000 units for digital print orders and 10,000-20,000 units for rotogravure print, depending on the pouch format, material structure, and complexity. We evaluate each project individually—if your target quantity is lower, reach out and we will explore what is possible. For brands just starting, we recommend our digital print option which eliminates cylinder costs and enables smaller batch runs.

Q: How does the sampling process work?

Once we agree on the material structure, dimensions, and print treatment, we produce samples that represent your production order. Digital print samples ship within 5-7 business days. If your project requires rotogravure printing (recommended for runs above 50,000 units), cylinder samples ship in 10-15 business days. Every sample includes the full laminate structure, closures, and print quality so you can validate barrier properties, seal integrity, and shelf appearance before committing to production.

Q: What is the typical production lead time?

Standard production lead time is 15-25 business days after sample approval and payment confirmation. Lead time varies based on order volume, material structure complexity, and current production schedule. Rush and partial shipments can be arranged—let us know your timeline when requesting a quote.

Q: Do you ship internationally? What shipping methods are available?

We ship to 50+ countries worldwide. Standard shipping is by sea freight (FCL for full containers, LCL for smaller volumes) with door-to-door service available in major markets. Air express is available for urgent orders. We handle all export documentation: commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, fumigation certificate, and FDA food-contact compliance letter. Incoterms: FOB, CIF, and DDP are all available.

Q: What payment terms do you offer?

Standard terms are 50% deposit with order confirmation and 50% before shipment. For established relationships and larger programs, we can discuss 30% deposit / 70% against shipping documents. We accept T/T (wire transfer) and Letter of Credit for qualified orders. Net terms may be available for repeat customers after an initial credit review.

Q: How does the custom packaging development process work from start to finish?

The process has six steps: (1) Discovery — you share product type, dimensions, quantity, barrier needs, and artwork; (2) Material Recommendation — we suggest laminate structures; (3) Artwork & Pre-Press — we review your design and provide a proof; (4) Sampling — digital or rotogravure samples are produced and shipped; (5) Production & QC — approved samples enter production with inline quality monitoring; (6) Final QC & Shipment — AQL inspection, packing, and export. Full details on our Custom Packaging page.

Q: What materials do you use for flexible packaging?

We work with a range of laminate structures: PET/AL/PE for maximum oxygen and moisture barrier, PET/VMPET/PE for metallized appearance with good barrier, NY/PE for puncture resistance and clarity, Kraft/AL/PE for a natural paper-touch exterior, and custom co-extrusions for specialized requirements. Material selection is driven by your product's barrier needs (OTR, WVTR), filling method, shelf-life target, and retail environment. See our Materials Guide for a full comparison.

Q: Can you print my existing artwork? What file formats do you accept?

Yes. We accept Adobe Illustrator (.ai), PDF, EPS, and high-resolution PSD files. Preferred specifications: CMYK color mode, 300 dpi minimum at 1:1 scale, 3 mm bleed, all fonts outlined, and Pantone color references noted. Our pre-press team reviews every file for print feasibility and provides feedback before cylinder engraving.

Q: What printing methods do you offer?

Rotogravure printing for production runs (up to 10 colors, exceptional color consistency, most cost-effective at scale) and digital HP Indigo printing for short runs, samples, and variable data. We can also combine methods—for example, rotogravure base colors with digital variable data like batch codes or regional labeling.

Q: What quality certifications do you hold?

Our facility operates under ISO 9001:2015 quality management and BRC Packaging certification. We comply with FDA 21 CFR for food-contact materials, EU 10/2011 for plastic food-contact migration, and REACH/ROHS. Third-party lab testing from SGS, Intertek, or TUV can be arranged for specific barrier, heavy metal, or migration requirements.

Q: Can you match my brand's Pantone colors?

Yes. Our rotogravure presses can print up to 10 colors with Pantone matching. We provide physical color drawdowns on your selected substrate for approval before cylinder engraving. Please provide Pantone references with your artwork files.

Q: What closures and add-on features are available?

Press-to-close zippers (single and double track), tear notches, laser scoring for easy-open, hang holes (round, sombrero, and euro-slot), one-way degassing valves (for coffee), clear windows (round, oval, full-side transparent), spout fitments (PP, top or corner position, tamper-evident caps), and child-resistant closures for regulated categories.

Q: How do you ensure quality control during production?

QC is integrated at every stage: incoming film inspection for gauge and visual defects, lamination bond strength testing, inline seal integrity and print registration monitoring during pouch making, visual defect sorting, and final AQL sampling per ISO 2859-1 before packing. We retain production samples for 12 months for traceability.

Q: Can you do child-resistant packaging?

Yes. We offer child-resistant zipper configurations and closure designs compliant with US 16 CFR 1700 and ISO 8317 standards. Common applications include cannabis edibles, laundry pods, and liquid nicotine refills. Please specify CR requirements during your project brief.

Q: How do I get a quote?

Visit our Contact page and share your product type, target dimensions, planned quantity, material or barrier preferences, and artwork concept. We respond within 24 hours with a tailored material recommendation, pricing estimate, and sampling timeline. You can also email us directly at sales@packlifybags.com.

Q: Do you offer eco-friendly or recyclable packaging options?

We offer mono-material PE/PE laminates that are polyethylene-based and compatible with store drop-off recycling programs in markets with PE film collection infrastructure. Post-consumer recycled (PCR) content options are available in select structures. We also offer paper-based laminates (Kraft/PLA and paper/PE) for brands targeting a renewable material profile. Note that recyclability and compostability claims depend on end-market infrastructure; we are transparent about the current limitations of flexible packaging circularity.
