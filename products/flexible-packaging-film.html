Why Packaging Film Works

Custom printed rollstock film is the foundation of high-volume flexible packaging lines. For brands with in-house or co-packer VFFS/HFFS operations, ordering custom-printed rollstock eliminates the need for pre-made pouch inventory, reduces warehousing costs, and enables just-in-time production scheduling.

  • Rollstock format for high-speed VFFS and HFFS automated filling lines
  • Custom-printed with rotogravure for vibrant, consistent brand graphics
  • Barrier laminates engineered for OTR, WVTR, and product compatibility
  • Eliminates pre-made pouch inventory; enables just-in-time production
  • Available as printed, unprinted, or combination (base print + digital overprint)

Optional Features

  • Hot-seal or cold-seal adhesive pattern
  • Eye-mark registration for VFFS detection
  • Matte or gloss finish with registered options
  • Tear notch or laser score pre-application
  • Easy-peel sealant layer
  • Static-dissipative treatment for dusty products
  • Barrier coating options (PVdC, AlOx, SiOx)

Printing Options

Rotogravure up to 10 colors with Pantone matching. Full-web print with registration marks at specified repeat intervals. Reverse-print on outer layer for scuff resistance. Cold seal pattern can be applied during the printing pass. Combination print available: rotogravure base graphics with digital overprint for variable data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum order quantity for printed packaging film?

Flexible MOQ from approximately 20,000 linear meters of printed rollstock. The exact number of finished packs depends on your bag dimensions and repeat length. We provide pack-count estimates based on your artwork and dimensions.

Do you supply unprinted rollstock?

Yes. We supply unprinted barrier laminates for line trials, co-packer testing, and brands that use in-house sleeve labeling. Unprinted rollstock minimums are lower than printed orders.

What information do you need to quote packaging film?

We need: product type, target barrier requirements (OTR/WVTR), film width, repeat length (if known), VFFS machine model, seal type (hot or cold), and approximate annual volume. Artwork is not required for an initial quote.

Can you apply cold seal for heat-sensitive products?

Yes. We apply cold seal adhesive in a registered pattern during the printing pass. Cold seal is ideal for chocolate, confectionery, and temperature-sensitive products that cannot tolerate heat-seal temperatures.
